celina bouscaillou
Interior architect & designer


I've recently graduated from an RNCP-accredited master's degree in interior architecture/design and I'm looking for a new experience. After completing a 4 month work placement and continuing my Masters in the same company on an apprenticeship, I was able to see the reality of an interior designer's tasks.

Whether in the field or in the studio, I was able to work on a variety of tasks, from sketches to site supervision, including making proposals to companies.

Receptive to the relationship between other forms of design, particularly graphic design, with architecture, I try to integrate them into my projects. The question of subjectivity in art is a subject that has particularly interested me in recent years. In particular, I've been able to witness it in my research during my undergraduate and postgraduate studies.

I've been wondering about and working more specifically on biomorphism and organic abstraction recently, and I've observed that the public generally has a very distinct idea about these styles of art. I chose to explore this theme for my dissertation, which led me to develop a hypothesis about the importance of having Nature's influence in one's habitat.
